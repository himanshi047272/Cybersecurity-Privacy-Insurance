# 📌 Overview

As the insurance industry increasingly adopts digital technologies, cloud computing, Artificial Intelligence (AI), and Internet of Things (IoT) devices, cybersecurity and data privacy have become critical concerns.

This research study investigates the cybersecurity and privacy challenges affecting **Life and Health Insurance Systems**, examining how technological advancement introduces new security risks while also creating opportunities for stronger digital protection.

The study reviews existing academic literature, identifies research gaps, and discusses future technologies that can improve cybersecurity resilience within the insurance sector.

---

# 🎯 Research Objectives

This study aims to:

- Identify major cybersecurity threats in Life & Health Insurance systems.
- Examine privacy concerns associated with AI-driven insurance services.
- Analyze how human, organizational, and technological factors contribute to cyber risks.
- Compare findings from existing academic literature.
- Propose future strategies for building secure and resilient insurance platforms.

# 🔑 Key Findings

The literature review highlights several major challenges:

- Increasing cyberattacks targeting healthcare and insurance organizations.
- Privacy concerns associated with AI-based decision-making.
- Legacy systems lacking modern cybersecurity protection.
- Human error remaining one of the leading causes of security breaches.
- Limited standardization across cybersecurity practices in insurance companies.
- Growing need for stronger governance and security policies.

The study concludes that cybersecurity should be approached through a combination of **technology, organizational policies, and human awareness** rather than relying on technical solutions alone.
